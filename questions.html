Frequently Asked Questions

1. How often should I service my air conditioning unit?

It's recommended to service your AC unit at least once or twice a year to maintain efficiency and prolong its lifespan. Regular maintenance includes cleaning filters, checking refrigerant levels, and inspecting components.

2. Why is my air conditioner not cooling properly?

Common reasons include dirty filters, refrigerant leaks, thermostat issues, or blocked vents. A professional inspection can help diagnose and fix the problem.

3. What are the signs that my AC needs repair?

Watch out for strange noises, weak airflow, warm air, water leaks, or high electricity bills. These could indicate issues that need immediate attention.

4. How can I reduce my AC‚Äôs energy consumption?

You can save energy by using a programmable thermostat, cleaning the filters regularly, sealing air leaks, and choosing an energy-efficient AC model.

5. What size air conditioner do I need for my space?

The right AC size depends on room size, insulation, and number of occupants. A professional assessment can help determine the best unit for your home or office.

6. How long does an air conditioner last?

With proper maintenance, an air conditioner can last 10 to 15 years. Regular servicing and timely repairs can extend its lifespan.

7. Is it necessary to clean air ducts?

Yes! Dirty air ducts can collect dust, mold, and allergens, affecting indoor air quality. It is recommended to clean them every 3 to 5 years.

8. Why does my AC emit a bad smell?

A foul odor may be caused by mold growth, clogged drains, or a dirty air filter. A thorough cleaning and maintenance check can eliminate the issue.

9. Can I install an air conditioner myself?

It is not recommended unless you are a professional. Incorrect installation can lead to performance issues, energy inefficiency, and potential safety hazards. Always hire a certified technician.
